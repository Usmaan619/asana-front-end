California Privacy Rights

As a California consumer, you possess the following rights concerning our use and disclosure of your Personal Data, subject to specific limitations under the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”):
• Right to know. You can request, up to twice in a 12-month period, specific details about the Personal Data we have gathered, utilized, disclosed, or sold about you in the previous 12 months, including:
• the categories and specific pieces of Personal Data collected about you;
• the sources from which we obtained the Personal Data;
• the business or commercial purposes behind collecting the Personal Data;
• the categories of third parties with whom we shared the Personal Data; and
• the categories of Personal Data about you that were disclosed for business purposes and sold to third parties, along with the categories of third parties receiving this information.
• Right to delete. You may request the deletion of Personal Data we have collected from you, subject to specific CCPA limitations.
• Right to opt-out of Personal Data sale. You retain the right to opt-out of the sale of your Personal Data. BHAI does not sell your Personal Data.
• Non-discrimination. The CCPA ensures that you will not face discrimination for exercising these rights.
To submit a request to exercise any of these rights, please email us at support@BHAI.finance. Before responding to your request, we will verify your identity by confirming that the email address from which you sent the request matches the one we have on record or by requiring you to log into your account.

Consumer Request by an Authorized Agent

If an authorized agent submits a consumer request under the CCPA on your behalf, we require the authorized agent to provide the following information to verify their authority to act on your behalf:
• Proof of authorization to represent the California consumer: (1) Authorization from the California Secretary of State, (2) Notarized written consent from the California consumer, or (3) Power of attorney.
• Proof of the California consumer's identity: (1) First and last name, (2) Email address, and (3) Password.

A CALIFORNIA RESIDENT WHO HAS PROVIDED PERSONAL DATA TO A BUSINESS WITH WHOM THEY HAVE ESTABLISHED A BUSINESS RELATIONSHIP FOR PERSONAL, FAMILY, OR HOUSEHOLD PURPOSES (A “CALIFORNIA CUSTOMER”) MAY REQUEST INFORMATION ABOUT WHETHER THE BUSINESS HAS DISCLOSED PERSONAL DATA TO ANY THIRD PARTIES FOR THE THIRD PARTIES’ DIRECT MARKETING PURPOSES. GENERALLY, IF THE BUSINESS HAS MADE SUCH A DISCLOSURE OF PERSONAL DATA, UPON RECEIPT OF A REQUEST BY A CALIFORNIA CUSTOMER, THE BUSINESS IS REQUIRED TO PROVIDE A LIST OF ALL THIRD PARTIES TO WHOM PERSONAL DATA WAS DISCLOSED IN THE PRECEDING CALENDAR YEAR, ALONG WITH A LIST OF THE CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA THAT WERE DISCLOSED. CALIFORNIA CUSTOMERS MAY REQUEST FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT OUR COMPLIANCE WITH THIS LAW BY EMAILING INFO@BHAIFINANCE.COM . PLEASE NOTE THAT WE ARE REQUIRED TO RESPOND TO ONE REQUEST PER CALIFORNIA CUSTOMER EACH YEAR AND WE ARE NOT REQUIRED TO RESPOND TO REQUESTS MADE THROUGH MEANS OTHER THAN THIS EMAIL ADDRESS.
